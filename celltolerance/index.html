The Science

How Food Intolerances Damage Your Gut

Atypical food allergies often go undetected by standard testing. Unlike classic allergies, these reactions cause localized damage to the tissue lining your gut — inflammation invisible to the naked eye, but visible with the right technology.

🔬

Cellular-Level Imaging

During a standard upper endoscopy, a specialized probe called Cellvizio® is used to visualize your gut tissue in real time at the cellular level — something conventional endoscopy cannot do.

🍳

Real-Time Food Challenges

Small amounts of suspected foods are applied directly to your intestinal lining. The probe then observes whether each food triggers a visible reaction — such as cellular swelling, gap formation, or tissue disruption.

🧠

Detecting Hidden Damage

Atypical food allergies cause localized damage to gut tissue that standard blood tests and skin prick tests cannot detect. CellTolerance® captures this damage as it happens, directly at the source.

✅

Clinically Meaningful Results

Patients who test positive for specific food intolerances are very likely to experience significant clinical improvement in their symptoms when they remove the identified trigger foods from their diet.

The Program

How the CellTolerance® Program Works

A comprehensive program that goes far beyond a single test — from precise diagnosis through personalized dietary support and long-term symptom relief.

1

Initial Consultation

Meet with our gastroenterology team to review your history, symptoms, and goals. We determine whether CellTolerance® testing is appropriate for your situation.

2

Upper Endoscopy with Cellvizio® Imaging

During a routine upper endoscopy under sedation, the advanced confocal laser probe is introduced to examine your intestinal tissue at the cellular level while specific foods are tested in real time.

3

Personalized Diet Plan

Based on your results, our registered dietitians create a tailored elimination plan — removing only the foods that triggered a reaction, so your diet stays as broad and enjoyable as possible.

4

Ongoing Support & Follow-Up

You will receive follow-up from our physicians and advanced practice providers, who will monitor your progress and adjust your plan as your symptoms improve.

Is It Right for You?

This Program May Be Helpful If You…

  • ✓Have been diagnosed with Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) and still don't have adequate symptom relief
  • ✓Suspect certain foods trigger your digestive symptoms but can't pinpoint which ones
  • ✓Have not found clear answers from other diets, tests, or treatments — even after ruling out celiac disease, allergies, and inflammatory bowel disease
  • ✓Experience bloating, abdominal pain, diarrhea, or nausea that seems connected to what you eat
  • ✓Feel frustrated by overly restrictive elimination diets that haven't provided lasting relief

Why a Positive Test Matters

Unlike conventional food sensitivity panels, CellTolerance® directly visualizes the damage that specific foods cause to your gut lining. This means a positive result isn't just a number on a lab report — it's objective evidence of tissue-level harm. Patients who receive a positive identification and subsequently avoid the indicated foods are very likely to experience meaningful, lasting improvement in their symptoms. The precision of this approach ensures you only eliminate what truly needs to go, making dietary changes manageable and sustainable.
